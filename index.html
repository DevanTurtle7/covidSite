COVID-19

Learn the Facts

About

COVID-19, also known as the Coronavirus is an infectious disease. Coronavirus causes respiratory problems such as damage to lung tissue and causes trouble breathing. COVID-19 is spread through droplets of saliva or other bodily fluids. These fluids most commonly are from coughs, sneezes or the nose.

Staying Safe

Frequently Wash Your Hands

  • Thoroughly wash your hands for at least 20 seconds with soap and hot water. If you don't have soap and water, you can also use hand sanitizer.
  • Washing your hands can prevent you from getting infected via surfaces.
  • It's important to wash your hands after commonly used surfaces and before eating or touching your face.

Wear a Mask

  • Wearing a mask can protect yourself and others from airborne spread of COVID-19
  • Masks should be worn at all times in public or whenever around others.
  • Wearing a mask is important even if you don't feel sick, since COVID-19 can be asymptomatic.

Socially Distance

  • Maintain a 6 foot distance with people when in public to minimize airborne spread.

Frequently Disinfect Surfaces

  • Disinfecting surfaces can kill any germs living on them and prevents spread.
  • Clean commonly used surfaces such as doorknobs, keyboards, tables, phones, faucets, etc.
  • Surfaces can be cleaned with soap, detergent, or household disinfectant.

Check for Symptoms

  • Being aware of your own health can is very important to protect yourself and others
  • Monitor your health daily. Check your temperature and be aware of any symptoms you may have.
  • Always preform safe practices such as wearing masks and social distancing even if you don't have any symptoms, as COVID-19 can be asymptomatic.
